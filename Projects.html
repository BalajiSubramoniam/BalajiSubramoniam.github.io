Past:

  1. Examples of Infinity Categories (Master's Dissertation)
    2. I wrote a Master's dissertation, guided by Prof. Neil Strickland and Prof. Sukhendu Mehrotra, on infinity categories. The beginning aim of the dissertation was to compare locally 0-truncated infinity categories and ordinary categories and simultaneously pick up the necessary elements of infinity categories. In hindsight, the problem in itself is fairly straightforward. However, laying the necessary groundwork to make this statement precise and prove (a few different versions of) the main statement is mildly tedious. Write to me if you would like a copy of the dissertation. In my opinion, you might possibly find the draft useful only in the following cases:
    1. You are interested in the rudiments of the theory of infinity categories (more precisely, quasicategories). However, I must concede that there are many better sources (See for example Groth's or Rezk's notes) that serve this purpose. In particular, I do not prove any of the standard results and in order to make the presentation streamlined, many integral results are left out altogether.
    2. You are interested in locally truncated ∞-categories.
    3. You are interested in certain definitions of "(∞,2)-Categories" (Weak ∞-bicategories, ∞-bicategories and (∞,2)-categories) and how they compare. This is discussed in papers of Lurie and Gagna, Harpaz and Lanari . The dissertation elaborates on some aspects of this along with a few proofs supplementing the literature.
